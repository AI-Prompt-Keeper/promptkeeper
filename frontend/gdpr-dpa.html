GDPR & Data Processing Agreement (DPA)

Last updated: February 2026

For customers in the European Union, European Economic Area, and United Kingdom, this page summarizes our compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and provides a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) framework.

1. GDPR Compliance Summary

Prompt Keeper is committed to complying with GDPR and UK GDPR when we process personal data of data subjects in the EU/EEA and UK. We act as a data processor when we process personal data on your behalf (e.g., storing your prompts and API keys, executing requests through our proxy). You typically act as the data controller for that data.

  • Lawful basis: We process personal data only as instructed by you and in accordance with our Terms and Privacy Policy.
  • Data subject rights: We assist you in responding to access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection requests where we process data on your behalf.
  • Security: We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures (see our Security Whitepaper).
  • Sub-processors: We use sub-processors (e.g., infrastructure, analytics) under written agreements that impose equivalent data protection obligations.
  • International transfers: Where we transfer personal data outside the EU/EEA/UK, we use approved mechanisms (e.g., Standard Contractual Clauses, adequacy decisions) as required.

2. Data Processing Agreement (DPA)

When our Terms or a separate order form require a DPA, the following terms apply to our processing of personal data on your behalf:

2.1 Roles

You are the data controller; Prompt Keeper is the data processor. We will process personal data only on your documented instructions (including via the Service and these Terms), unless required by law.

2.2 Confidentiality and Security

We ensure that persons authorized to process personal data are bound by confidentiality and that we implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk (e.g., encryption, access controls, monitoring).

2.3 Sub-processors

We may engage sub-processors to support the Service. We maintain a list of sub-processors and will inform you of changes. We impose on sub-processors the same data protection obligations as in this DPA. You may object to a new sub-processor on reasonable grounds; we will work in good faith to address your concerns or offer alternatives.

2.4 Data Subject Rights

We will assist you in fulfilling requests from data subjects (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection) to the extent we process the relevant data and as reasonably required by applicable law.

2.5 Breach Notification

We will notify you without undue delay after becoming aware of a personal data breach affecting your data and will provide information reasonably necessary for you to meet your breach notification obligations.

2.6 Deletion and Return

Upon termination of the Service or at your request, we will delete or return personal data in accordance with your instructions and our retention policy, unless we are required to retain it by law.

2.7 Audits

We will make available to you information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA. You may request an audit only where required by a supervisory authority or where our self-service information is insufficient; we may require an NDA and reasonable notice, and audits will be at your expense unless otherwise agreed.

2.8 Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)

Where we transfer personal data from the EU/EEA/UK to a country not recognized as providing an adequate level of protection, we will implement the EU Standard Contractual Clauses (and UK addendum where applicable) or other approved transfer mechanisms. Our DPA supplement or order form may incorporate these by reference.

3. Contact

For DPA requests, GDPR-related questions, or to execute a signed DPA: privacy@promptkeeper.example or legal@promptkeeper.example. We can provide a signable DPA and sub-processor list upon request.
